Question 1

Consider the credit.csv file where balance (average credit card debt for a number of individuals) is the output attribute. Quantitative input attributes are age, cards (number of credit cards), education (years of education), income (in thousands of dollars), limit (credit limit), and rating (credit rating). Qualitative input attributes are gender, student, married, and ethnicity (Caucasian, African American or Asian).

 

 

a.      Using linear regression, investigate whether gender has an effect on the credit card balance. What is the average credit card debt for males and females?

 

Balance ~ Gender

P_value of F-test of the model = 0.6685. High p value means that we fail to reject that mean values of Balance w.r.t Gender are same. Therefore, there is no significant difference between the mean values of Balance w.r.t Gender. In other words, gender does not have an effect on the credit card balance.

The average credit card debt for males: the intercept of the model, that is equals to 509.8031.

The average credit card debt for females: the intercept + coefficient of GenderFemale = 529.5362

 

b.      Using linear regression, investigate whether ethnicity has an effect on the credit card balance.

 

Balance ~ Ethnicity

p_value of F-test of the model = 0.9575. High p value means that we fail to reject that mean values of Balance w.r.t Ethnicity are same. Therefore, there is no significant difference between the mean values of Balance w.r.t Ethnicity. In other words, ethnicity does not have an effect on the credit card balance.

 

c.       We want to investigate whether the effect of income on balance is different for a student compared to a non-student. How can we do this?

 

Balance ~ Student * Income

To investigate whether the effect of income on balance is different for a student compared to a non-student, the interaction between Student and Income should be considered.

The interaction term seems to be insignificant. Therefore, the effect of income on balance is not different for a student compared to a non-student.

Yet, it would be wiser to look at the interaction effect on the graph. The graph below shows that the slopes for StudentNo and StudentYes are different. However, this difference is because of the large income instances. Although they cause difference in slopes, the confidence intervals around the blue and orange lines overlaps.

d.      Perform linear regression first only with input attributes age and limit, and then with only rating and limit. What are your conclusions?

 

The Comparison of the two models

Model1 considers Age and Limit to estimate Balance. The p value of the F test of the model is quite small. Therefore, this model is a valid model. Moreover, since their p values are less than 0.05, both Ae and Limit emerge as significant factors.

Model2 considers Rating and Limit to estimate Balance. The p value of the F test of the model is quite small. Therefore, this model is a valid model. However, p-value corresponding to Limit is larger than 0.05. This means that Limit does not have a significant effect on Balance when it is considered with Rating. Although one of the factors become insignificant, R_squared slightly decreases. This causes a suspicion about the correlated factors.

The correlation between Limit and Rating is 0.997. Therefore, p values of the factors are relatively high but the R_squared remains almost the same. On the other hand, the correlation between Limit and Age is 0.1. Therefore model 1 is more reliable.
